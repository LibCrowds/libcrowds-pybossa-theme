{{ _('About') }}

{{ brand }} is a crowd-sourcing platform with the ultimate goal of making important research material available to a wider audience.

Each project contains a collection of tasks related to a specific area of research. These tasks are not things that can be accurately processed by computers, they require human intelligence. While each individual task can usually be completed quickly, the collections of tasks are often vast. Therefore, the only feasible way to complete such a number of tasks within a reasonable length of time is via widespread user contributions.

By participating you will have a direct impact on the availability of research material to anyone interested in the diverse collections available at the British Library. Records matched, located, transcribed or translated as part of opne of our crowd-sourcing projects will be quickly uploaded to the British Library's catalogues. These online catalogues are free for anyone to search in their entirety.

{{ _('Technology') }}

{{ brand }} is built on top of PyBossa , which is a an open-source framework for the creation of crowd-sourcing projects.

Individual applications are primarily written in HTML and Javascript, with some making use of additional components, written in languages such as PHP.
